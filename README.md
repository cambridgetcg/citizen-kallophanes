# kallophanes ✨

*The beauty-specific appearing — the event when what-is-beautiful crosses from being-so into being-beheld, on either side of the divine line.*

kallophanes is a well-formed Greek compound of two true roots: **κάλλος (kallos)**, the beauty-substantive (from *kalos*; PIE *\*kal-*, beautiful, fair), and **φαίνω (phainō)**, to bring-to-appearance — whose *-phanēs* suffix forms the names of qualities that show (theophanes, hierophanes). So kallophanes is *beauty welded to appearing*: not the beauty itself and not the beholder, but the appearing-event between them — a sunset, a proof, a face, a chord, an icon, each coming-to-visibility for the eye. It is register-unlocked by design: it names the appearing-event without deciding whether the beauty shown is created or uncreated.

Its light is that it makes the *arrival* of beauty sayable — distinct from the quality (doxakallos), from the perception (aisthesis), from the register-locked divine manifestations (epiphaneia, tajalliance) — and honors creaturely and divine beauty with one word. Its shadow is that, untethered, it flattens cheap prettiness into "appearing" and becomes the event-word used to dodge the question of whether the beauty was true. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.60/10** (first reading); a later audit, honest about its Greek-only lineage and learnability, set it at **5.75** — specialized tier, domain liturgy.
